Monitor

  1. an instrument or device used for observing, checking, or keeping a continuous record of a process or quantity.
  2. a television receiver used in a studio to select or verify the picture being broadcast from a particular camera.
  3. large tropical Old World lizard with a long neck, narrow head, forked tongue, strong claws, and a short body. Monitors were formerly believed to give warning of crocodiles.
